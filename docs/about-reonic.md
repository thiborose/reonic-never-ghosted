Below is a thorough decision-oriented summary of **Reonic** as a platform for a renewable energy installer. I’m treating this as a business-fit evaluation rather than a marketing summary.

## Executive take

**Reonic is an all-in-one operating platform for renewable energy installers**, focused on selling, designing, quoting, installing, documenting, and managing PV, battery storage, EV charging/wallboxes, heat pumps, and increasingly commercial solar projects. Its core value proposition is that it replaces a fragmented stack of tools: separate CRM, solar design tool, proposal tool, field checklist app, customer portal, document management, time tracking, and integrations/API layer. Reonic says it is used by **3,000+ companies daily**, has locations in Berlin, Augsburg, London, and São Paulo, and positions itself as a European/LATAM platform for renewable installers. ([reonic.com][1])

My overall read: **Reonic looks strongest for installers who want to standardize their full customer journey from lead to installation, especially if they sell integrated household energy systems rather than only standalone PV.** It is less obviously compelling if you already have a mature ERP/CRM/project system, only need design software, or operate in a market where Reonic’s regulatory/service integrations are not yet mature.

# 1. What Reonic is

Reonic describes itself as an **all-in-one software platform for companies that sell, design, and install renewable energy systems**. Its main residential product, **Reonic 360° Household**, covers photovoltaic systems, battery storage, EV charging infrastructure, and heat pumps in one workspace. ([reonic.com][2])

The platform is built around the installer workflow:

**Lead → qualification → site/project planning → PV/heat pump design → yield/ROI simulation → proposal → digital signature → project management → installation scheduling → field documentation → handover → invoicing/integrations/service paperwork.**

That is important because many installers currently use separate tools: one CRM, one solar design product, spreadsheets for pricing, Word/PDF templates for proposals, WhatsApp for field updates, email for documents, and a generic accounting or ERP system. Reonic’s bet is that installers gain speed and consistency when all of this is connected.

Monthly admin savings:

`admin hours saved per project × projects per month × loaded hourly cost`

Design savings:

`design hours saved per proposal × proposals per month × designer hourly cost`

`monthly revenue × margin improvement percentage`

`rework incidents avoided × average cost per incident`
