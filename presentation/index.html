The one-line takeaway

Do not demo "AI writes follow-up emails". Demo "AI tells the installer what to do next, why, when, and how to learn from it."

The strongest product shape is a Germany-aware Next Best Action assistant embedded in the quoted-customer workflow.

diagnose blocker choose action check consent check claims schedule debrief

Evidence: A0 Reonic workflow, A3 objection routing, A4 Germany market/compliance, A6 action/debrief, A7 product synthesis.

Demo user

Installer scenario: owner-led team with 20 active quoted leads.

Installer reality

  • Owner handles sales, calls, site visits, and operations.
  • Has paid for leads but cannot follow up deeply.
  • Needs help deciding where time is worth spending.
  • Cannot afford generic sequences that annoy customers.

Assistant promise

  • "This customer is not cold; they are stuck on ROI."
  • "Do not call; phone consent is missing."
  • "Send an itemized addendum, then book a 15-minute review."
  • "After the call, capture the objection in 20 seconds."
Key finding 1

Personas should be motive and blocker scores, not stereotypes.

Old idea Research-backed model PoC behavior
Investor ROI and cost-control score Assumptions, payback range, comparison matrix.
Environmentalist Climate motive when explicit CO2 card plus economics, source-blocked if missing.
Skeptic Risk/trust blocker Named owner, proof, no-pressure action.
Family Explicit stakeholder review state Shareable summary only when partner/advisor is mentioned.

Evidence: Agent 1 persona segmentation, Agent 2 VOC, Agent 5 ethics guardrails.

Key finding 2

The blockers are operational, not just emotional.

Offer blockers

Unclear line items, bundled scope, exclusions, variants, assumptions.

Economic blockers

Upfront price, liquidity, payback distrust, competitor quotes.

Technical blockers

Battery sizing, winter output, heat-pump monthly burden, roof risk.

Trust blockers

Pressure, slow response, unclear owner, vague responsibility.

Admin blockers

VNB, grid, MaStR, meter, subsidy, financing, timeline.

Process blockers

Co-decision-maker, stale review, no consent, no value-add.

Example 1

ROI and competitor comparison: the assistant should not send a generic reminder.

Customer signal

Munich homeowner has a PV + battery quote, uploaded bill, and mentioned two competitor offers.

ROI high competitor active phone consent yes

Recommended action

Create an assumptions table and comparison addendum, then schedule a 15-minute ROI review.

PoC feature: one-click "ROI review" action with editable tariff, feed-in, self-consumption, and comparison checklist.

Example 2

Battery/autarky: do not force ROI-only messaging.

Customer signal

Hamburg homeowner wants independence but asks whether a 12 kWh battery is oversized.

autarky high battery doubt WhatsApp opt-in

Recommended action

Generate "battery now vs battery-ready later", show realistic self-consumption range, use WhatsApp only for logistics.

PoC feature: scenario comparison that respects nonfinancial independence motive.

Example 3

PV + heat pump: missing data should block confident claims.

Customer signal

NRW homeowner worries the heat pump will increase winter electricity bills. Heat-load inputs are missing.

winter concern heat-load missing claim blocked

Recommended action

Request bill and heat-load data, then schedule a virtual consultation with seasonal/monthly scenario.

PoC feature: "claim blocked" state that feels trustworthy, not limiting.

Example 4

Roof/trust risk: sometimes the next best action is a visit, but only when justified.

Customer signal

Older roof, customer asks who pays if tiles break. No phone consent.

roof risk trust repair phone blocked

Recommended action

Email inspection checklist and warranty/insurance boundaries. Offer a site visit with travel impact shown.

PoC feature: visit ROI card with "what this visit unlocks".

Key finding 3

Germany-specific gates are a demo advantage.

Channel gate

  • Phone, SMS, WhatsApp, email, voicemail: check consent and purpose.
  • Tracking: do not use opens/clicks unless tracking consent exists.

Claim gate

  • ROI, feed-in, subsidy, finance, tax, timelines: source and checked date.
  • If stale or missing, create an internal task instead of unsafe copy.

Evidence: Agent 4 Germany market/economics/compliance; Agent 5 guardrails; Agent 6 channel playbook.

Anti-scope

What not to build this weekend

No generic campaign builder

It undersells the research and looks like every CRM automation tool.

No black-box close score

Predictive scores are unvalidated. Use transparent hypotheses.

No fake local proof

Use permissioned placeholders or explicitly mark demo proof.

No unsupported ROI certainty

Source/date everything or block the claim.

No broad persona stereotypes

Use stated motives and blockers.

No visit-by-default logic

Visits are expensive for small installers. Justify travel.

Final product thesis

The demo should make Reonic feel like the installer's quote-closing operating system.

"For this customer, do this next, because this is the blocker, this channel is allowed, this claim is safe, this slot fits your calendar, and this is what we learned afterward."

That is the clearest, research-backed way to move from quote sent to contract signed in a 48-hour PoC.
