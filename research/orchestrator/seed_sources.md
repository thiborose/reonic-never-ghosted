- KfW Energiewendebarometer via pv magazine: https://www.pv-magazine.de/2024/09/12/photovoltaik-anlagen-sind-fuer-haushalte-der-favorit-unter-den-energiewendetechnologien/
- Solarwatt/Appinio market study via pv magazine: https://www.pv-magazine.de/2023/04/03/solarwatt-marktstudie-photovoltaik-hoch-im-kurs-bei-hauseigentuemern/
- EUPD SolarProsumerMonitor source signal: https://eupd-group.com/eupd-research-publishes-electrical-energy-storage-report-europe-h2-2024/

Notes from source discovery:

- E.ON/Statista surveyed 2,000 German homeowners living in their own houses; pv magazine reports cost saving, climate protection, property value, autarky, and powering heat pumps/EVs as major motives.
- Wegatech/YouGov surveyed 1,015 homeowners aged 25-65 with at least 3,000 EUR household net income; source highlights energy-cost shock, autarky, high upfront cost, building constraints, and profitability concerns.
- KfW Energiewendebarometer summary indicates PV, batteries, heat pumps, and EVs are increasingly combined in German households, but affordability remains a major barrier.
- Solarwatt/Appinio surveyed 1,000 German homeowners and found cost saving and energy-market independence ahead of environmental motivation.
