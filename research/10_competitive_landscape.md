# 10 Competitive Landscape

Status: completed bounded synthesis on 2026-06-20.

## Competitive Categories

| Category | Example vendors | Public positioning | Reonic assistant implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| End-to-end renewable installer ERP | Reonic, OpusFlow, Sollit | CRM, quoting, planning, operations, inventory/service, finance/admin | A marketing assistant must integrate into workflow and tasks, not sit outside as copywriter |
| Quotation and proposal software | Solar Monkey, Reonic proposal workflow | Accurate calculations, compelling proposals, option comparison, customer approval | Proposal clarity and assumptions are conversion levers |
| Heat-pump planning OS | autarc, Reonic heat-pump planning | LiDAR/remote data capture, heating load, hydraulic balancing, subsidies, financing, reports | Heat-pump quote follow-up needs building-readiness and monthly-burden proof |
| Customer portal and referral/review platforms | Sunvoy, Reonic customer portal | Customer project tracking, documents, reviews, referrals, service tickets | Aftercare proof, support expectations, and local social proof matter |
| Generic CRM/marketing automation | HubSpot/Salesforce-style tools, installer-specific CRM add-ons | Sequences, tasks, reporting, lead management | Differentiation comes from renewable-specific data, claim checks, consent, and proof |

## Observed Competitive Messages

- OpusFlow presents the problem as disconnected sales, operations, planning, warehouse, and finance for sustainable installers.
- Solar Monkey centers accurate calculations, compelling quotations, option comparison, and trust.
- autarc centers AI-supported energy sales/planning, heat-pump data capture, financing, subsidies, and mandatory reports.
- Sollit centers one software platform from lead to design, installation, and service.
- Sunvoy centers customer portal, reviews/referrals, operations, documents, tickets, and monitoring.

## White Space For This PoC

The strongest differentiator is not "AI email generation." The white space is a Germany-aware post-quote assistant that:

- Detects the real blocker from quote data, CRM notes, customer language, and missing proof.
- Chooses among proposal revision, ROI review, finance explanation, document request, call, video, visit, pause, or escalation.
- Gates outreach by German consent and tracking requirements.
- Gates claims by current tariffs, subsidy/finance facts, assumptions, and source dates.
- Schedules the action around a small installer's limited capacity.
- Captures debrief and learns which actions actually move quotes to signed contracts.

## Competitive Risks

- Competitors already claim end-to-end workflows and AI. A PoC that only generates email copy will look shallow.
- Proposal software competitors already emphasize trust through accurate calculations, so Reonic must show the "why this action now" layer.
- Heat-pump tools emphasize field data capture; Reonic follow-up must know when data is missing and avoid unsupported heat-pump claims.
- Customer-portal tools emphasize reviews/referrals; Reonic should require real, permissioned, comparable proof assets.
