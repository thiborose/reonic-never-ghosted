# 16 Open Questions

Status: completed synthesis on 2026-06-20.

## Highest Priority

| Question | Why it matters | Evidence needed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What Reonic fields are available for quote, option, signature, portal, communication, consent, and calendar? | Determines implementation feasibility | Private schema/API review |
| Does Reonic store channel consent and tracking consent with source/timestamp/scope? | Required for Germany-compliant outreach | Legal/product data review |
| Which objection categories are most common in actual Reonic-connected sales notes and lost deals? | Public sources cannot rank prevalence | CRM notes, debriefs, lost reasons |
| Which actions correlate with signed contracts after quote? | Needed for predictive scoring and cadence | Outcome-linked action history |
| How should legal classify quote-service follow-up vs advertising in Germany? | Determines channel eligibility | Counsel review of exact flows |
| What minimum debrief will small installers actually complete? | Learning loop depends on adoption | Usability tests and installer interviews |

## Medium Priority

| Question | Why it matters | Evidence needed |
| --- | --- | --- |

## Current-Fact Validation

- Local subsidies by postcode/municipality.
- Financing rates and partner eligibility.
- VNB/grid process by customer location.
- Actual customer tariff and heat-pump tariff.
- Smart meter/control/export-limit status.
- Current tax/VAT eligibility for the specific system and customer.

## Product Risks To Keep Visible

- The assistant becomes a pressure engine.
- The assistant invents certainty around ROI, subsidies, finance, or timelines.
- The assistant overfits persona labels and ignores explicit customer notes.
- The assistant recommends actions the installer cannot execute.
- The assistant cannot learn because debrief is skipped.
