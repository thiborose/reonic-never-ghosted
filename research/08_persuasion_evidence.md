# 08 Persuasion Evidence

Status: completed synthesis on 2026-06-20.

## Product-Ready Principles

| Principle | Evidence | Confidence | Product behavior |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Lead with trust and risk reduction | A5, A2 | Medium-high | Show competence, reliability, availability, scope clarity, warranties, local accountability |
| Use authentic local proof only | A5, A2 | Medium | Store proof asset source, permission, location, project similarity, and checked date |
| Help homeowners compare and verify | A5, A2, A3 | High | Offer comparison checklist, itemized proposal, assumptions, exclusions, payment milestones |
| Use balanced objection handling | A5 | Medium | Acknowledge concern, show assumption, explain mitigation, offer verification |
| Reduce choice overload | A5 | Medium | Present one recommended path plus a small number of alternatives and expandable detail |
| Use concrete next-step planning | A5, A6 | Medium | Schedule review, partner call, document upload, technical clarification, or visit with agenda |
| Respond fast to customer-initiated events | A5, A6 | Medium | Prioritize inbound questions and promised follow-ups; do not pressure after quote |
| Cap stale follow-up | A5, A6 | Medium-low | Pause or switch to value-add only after repeated no-response |
| Test contract-relevant outcomes | A5 | High | Optimize next-step booked, completed action, signed contract, lost reason, opt-out, complaint |

## Ethical Red Lines

- No artificial urgency, scarcity, or fear.
- No "free solar", "no-cost", guaranteed bill elimination, guaranteed payback, or unsupported government/utility endorsement.
- No fabricated reviews, references, rankings, customer stories, or neighborhood popularity.
- No non-consented calls, SMS, WhatsApp, email, voicemail, or tracking.
- No manipulation based on sensitive or inferred vulnerability traits.
- No hidden assumptions or exclusions for ROI, tariffs, subsidy, finance, warranty, maintenance, grid connection, or incentives.

## Recommended A/B Test Candidates

| Hypothesis | Segment | Primary metric | Guardrails |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Balanced objection response beats one-sided reassurance | Technical/trust skeptics | Review call booked, signed contract | Complaint, opt-out, negative debrief |
| Recommended-plan summary beats full option dump | Undecided/busy prospects | Proposal review, booked next step | Requests for detail/confusion |
| Local verified proof asset improves trust repair | Trust-risk prospects | Reply or call booked | Privacy, proof accuracy |
| Scheduled next-step prompt beats generic follow-up | Eligible quote-stage leads | Calendar booking, completed call | Cancellation, opt-out |
| Consent-aware multi-channel beats email-only | Leads with consented channels | Qualified next action, signed contract | Consent violations, complaints |

## Product Positioning

The defensible approach is decision support, not persuasion pressure. The assistant should help the installer reduce uncertainty, explain why the action fits the customer's stated blocker, and create a concrete next step the customer can accept, adjust, or decline.
